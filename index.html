🧠 How It Works

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation to estimate your maintenance calories (TDEE).


1. Calculate BMR:

BMR = 66 + (6.23 * weight) + (12.7 * height) - (6.8 * age) BMR = 655 + (4.35 * weight) + (4.7 * height) - (4.7 * age)

2. Apply Activity Factor:

Who this is for:
People who are mostly sitting during the day and don’t work out at all.

Examples:
🔹 Desk job, remote worker, or student who sits most of the day
🔹 Minimal walking or movement outside of daily tasks (e.g., making meals, showering)

Good fit for:
Someone just starting their fitness journey with GD Fitness and hasn’t begun any structured physical activity yet. Who this is for:
You do light exercise 1–3 times a week, or you walk a bit more than average in your daily life.

Examples:
🔹 Walking your dog every day
🔹 A couple of yoga classes or short workouts per week
🔹 Light household chores or errands

Good fit for:
New GD Fitness users who are easing into a healthy routine —
maybe doing beginner classes or trying out gym sessions a few times per week. Who this is for:
You exercise 3–5 days a week consistently with moderate intensity.

Examples:
🔹 Following a workout plan from GD Fitness 3–5 days a week
🔹 Doing strength training, group fitness classes, or cardio regularly
🔹 Working a job that includes light movement (e.g., retail)

Good fit for:
Members in your first or second month at GD Fitness who’ve committed to a structured fitness program. Who this is for:
You work out 6–7 days a week, often with high intensity, or you have a physically demanding job.

Examples:
🔹 Daily lifting, HIIT, or fitness classes at GD Fitness
🔹 Active lifestyle outside the gym — cycling, running, or playing sports
🔹 Jobs like delivery, warehouse work, or construction

Good fit for:
Regular gymgoers, fitness class lovers, or members training for a fitness goal (like fat loss or muscle building). Who this is for:
You’re training intensely every day and might have a physically demanding job or a second workout per day.

Examples:
🔹 Two-a-day training sessions (e.g., lifting in the morning, cardio at night)
🔹 Athletes, personal trainers, or those training for a competition
🔹 Jobs with heavy physical labor (e.g., movers, firefighters)

Good fit for:
GD Fitness members pushing their limits or training for something big —
like a physique show, marathon, or transformation challenge. 🔹When in doubt, choose a slightly lower activity level.

🔹It’s easier to increase your calories later than to cut them if your estimate is too high.

TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor

This gives you the estimated calories to maintain your weight.
